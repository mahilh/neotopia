## SECTION 1 — WHAT IT IS

**Heading:** A strategy game with a 29-year horizon

**Body:**
NeoTopia is a browser multiplayer strategy game set in the year 2055. You and up to three others build a civilization together — placing elements across a hexagonal board, completing patterns, and scoring civilization districts.

No luck mechanics. No randomness. No dice. Every outcome is the direct consequence of a decision. The game is pure strategy, which means every win is earned and every loss is a lesson.

The board is a hexagonal city divided into three living districts: Sacred City, Living Earth, and Free Energy. Your moves leave permanent marks. Your scored buildings join a global index of every district ever constructed across all NeoTopia games ever played.

---

## SECTION 2 — HOW IT WORKS

**Heading:** Three actions. One turn. Build or fall behind.

**Step 1:** Draw a project card from the offer or deck. Your hand holds the patterns you are working toward.

**Step 2:** Move an element from a factory into one of the three districts. When your placed element completes a pattern matching a card in your hand, you score that district — immediately.

**Step 3:** Manage the factories. When a factory empties, it refills from the production stack. When the last tile in the stack is revealed, the end of the civilization cycle begins.

**The Diverse City rule:** You cannot build the same type of structure twice in a row in the same region. Variety is not optional. It is civilizational law.

**The tension mechanic:** Your worst region score is multiplied by three in the final tally. You cannot ignore a failing district. Balance is not strategy — it is survival.
