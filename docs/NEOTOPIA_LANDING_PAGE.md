## HOW IT WORKS SECTION

**Section label:** The Game

**Headline:**
Three actions per turn. That's it.

**Body:**
NeoTopia is a hexagonal strategy game set in the year 2055.
You and up to three others build a consciousness civilization — placing elements, completing patterns, and scoring districts.

Each turn you choose three times: draw a project card, or move an element from a factory into one of three living regions. When your placed element completes a pattern matching one of your cards, you score that building.

No luck mechanics. No randomness. Every outcome is a decision.

**Three micro-cards below:**

Card 1 — PLACE
Move elements from factories into regions.
Build patterns. Complete districts.

Card 2 — SCORE
Match your hand to the board.
Each completed pattern builds a named civilization project.

Card 3 — BALANCE
Your worst region's score is multiplied by three.
You cannot ignore a failing district. Balance is civilizational law.

## THE PSYCHOLOGY SECTION (for people who read deeper)

**Section label:** Why it feels different

**Headline:**
Pure strategy changes what it means to lose.

**Body:**
In games with dice, losses are absorbed by randomness. You couldn't help it. The game ate your turn.

In NeoTopia there are no dice. No random resource rolls. No luck mechanics of any kind. Every outcome — every missed pattern, every suboptimal placement, every region you let fall behind — is a decision you made.

This is harder. It is also more satisfying. When you win, you actually won. When you lose, you know what to study.

The worst region score is multiplied by three in final scoring. This single mechanic forces every player to care about their entire civilization, not just their strongest district. It weaponizes loss aversion in service of balance. You feel it in your stomach every time you check the score track.

That feeling is the game working.
