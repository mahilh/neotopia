## WHO IS NEOTOPIA FOR

NeoTopia has three audiences who never overlap in a typical pitch but all belong here:

**Audience 1: Strategy game players**
  They want pure strategy, no luck, depth, replayability.
  They compare to Catan, chess, Go, Colonist.io.
  They play on mobile. They play globally.
  They don't care about consciousness.

**Audience 2: Conscious entrepreneurs / solarpunk community**
  They want a game that aligns with their values.
  They're tired of games that simulate war, empire, extraction.
  They want to build something meaningful.
  They care deeply about 2055.

**Audience 3: Institutional / city planners / systems designers**
  They want a training tool for civilization-scale thinking.
  They use games for policy exploration.
  They're interested in the Global Index as a data source.
  They see NeoTopia as a planning oracle.

## PITCH BY AUDIENCE

### For Strategy Game Players:

"This is the game Catan players have been waiting for. Pure strategy — no dice, no luck, no randomness. Two players compete to build the highest-scoring civilization using 56 cards across four resource types. Every decision is yours. The scoring formula rewards balance — neglecting any element costs you three times. It's chess with territory.

Colonist.io has 2 million players. We're building the game they graduate to."

### For Conscious Entrepreneurs:

"NeoTopia is the first strategy game where every card you play is a rehearsal for a real building that will exist by 2055. The Free Energy Lab card? That's Tesla's vision, playable. The Food Forest? You're placing it in a digital city now and a real neighborhood later.

Every game contributes to the Global Civilization Index — a permanent record of the collective intention of everyone who plays. When 10,000 people score the Healing Arts Center highest in their games, that data tells us what to build first in the real district.

The game is the civilization in its holographic phase."

### For Institutional Partners:

"NeoTopia is a civilization-design simulation with measurable outcomes. Players must balance four resource types (Energy, BioFarming, Technology, Community) with a scoring formula that penalizes neglect tripled. Our research shows that players who complete 10 games develop significantly more holistic systems thinking — they stop optimizing for one variable.

The Global Civilization Index aggregates game scores across all players, creating a real-time dataset of which civilization elements communities prioritize. For urban planners and policy designers, this is a planning oracle that collects through play.

We're developing a corporate edition for team training in systems thinking and collaborative strategy."

## THE COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

**Colonist.io** — Most direct competitor. Online Catan implementation. 2M+ players.
  Our advantage: pure strategy (no dice) + consciousness theme + mobile-first + deeper scoring
  Their advantage: first mover · established community · Catan IP familiarity

**Civilization VI** — Turn-based strategy. Single player mostly.
  Our advantage: multiplayer-first · pure browser · no download · consciousness theme · real-world link
  Their advantage: 30 years of brand · massive content · deep feature set

**Tabletopia / Board Game Arena** — Digital board game platforms
  Our advantage: original game · no licensing needed · custom mechanics · web-native
  Their advantage: existing game libraries · large communities

**No competitor** has:
  - Pure strategy (zero luck) as a core feature
  - A consciousness/civilization building theme
  - A Global Index that connects to real-world outcomes
  - A scoring formula that encodes systems-thinking principles
  - The mystery school layer (sacred milestones)
